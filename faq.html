Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is an NFT?

    NFT stands for Non Fungable Token. For our application, NFTs are modern day collectables. We are in essence publishing your little one's art as a digital scrapbook and stamping it with metadata for authenticity. Much like baseball cards, their value appreciated along with the players' career progression. NFTs have similar appreciation potential, so or goal is to turn your little one's art into an asset with the chance to appreciate.

  • What is the future of NFTs?

    NFTs have been around since 2014 and became extremly popular in 2021, with that popularity some NFTs from notable people were selling for millions.
    There is currently a downturn of this speculative value, however it is giving room for the techonolgy itself to shine. Brands and bands are using NFTs, artists and entertainers are using them, each in their own unique ways. At Scrapnook we believe our application of this technology is where it shines the most. We don't know where it will lead, but we do know NFT technology will be in our future.

  • Some technical info on the process.

    We will be minting the NFTs onto the Solana blockchain. This network is popular related to its affordable, speed and scalability. There are many talented coders working with the Solana blockchain. Positioning our NFTs in this ecosystem will give our clients the best chance to get the most from their NFTs. We use some well supported libraries to mint onto the blockchain and some custom made software to write all the data needed to interact with these libraries. We plan to get our process audited by third party specialists until then we are minting the NFTs as mutable. This means that if there are any issues with the process we can fix the NFT quickly and easily. Once our process has been audited we will lock the existing NFTs and mint future NFTs and non-mutable.
