Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the Blockchain?

    The blockchain is an online ledger. A series of data blocks with a record of transactions within it, for example - Wallet A paid Wallet B $5. The currency that transacts is the coin associated with that blockchain. There are millions of transactions per day and each block is linked together and visible to all. These online ledgers are stored on multiple computers across the network making it impossible for any one organization to control or manipulate

  • What is an NFT?

    NFT stands for Non-Fungible-Token. Some very smart people in 2014, figured out that they could write some code and insert some data into the blockchain. They linked that piece of data to a video they owned (photo and video files can be used). This action is called minting. By minting this video onto the blockchain it meant that anyone who had the address of this mint could look it up and play the video. They in essence published this video online with a signature of ownership very much like an artist displays work in a gallery with their signature at the bottom

  • Some technical info on the process.

    We will be minting the NFTs onto the Solana blockchain. This network is popular related of its affordable, speed, and scalability. Many talented coders are working on the Solana blockchain and positioning our NFTs in this ecosystem will give our clients the best chance to get the most from their NFTs. We use well-supported libraries to mint onto the blockchain and custom-made software to write all the data needed to interact with these libraries. We use Arweave storage solutions along with our smart contract to complete the NFT package. We plan to get our process audited by third-party specialists to ensure our workflow is flawless and remains up to date. Until then we are minting the NFTs as mutable. This means that if there are any issues with the process, we can fix the NFT quickly and easily. Once our process has been audited, we will lock the existing NFTs and mint future NFTs and non-mutable ones.
