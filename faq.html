Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the Blockchain?

    First, a blockchain exists for each cryptocurrency. Each blockchain is a list of transactions grouped in blocks and linked together by addresses. Each transaction is a simple send and receive record like account A sent account B $5. All this data on this blockchain is duplicated and distributed across multiple systems which creates a network with more than one source of truth. This is important because it makes records impossible to change, hack or manipulate. Most blockchains are public and every transaction can be traced back to its origin wallet.

  • What is an NFT?

    NFT stands for Non-Fungible-Token. Some very smart people in 2014, figured out that they could write some code and insert some data into the blockchain. They linked that piece of data to one of their homemade videos (photo and video files can be used). This action is called minting. By minting this video onto the blockchain it meant that anyone who had their address could look it up and play the video. They in essence published this video online with a signature of ownership very much like an artist displays work in a gallery with their signature on the bottom.

  • What are the benifits?

At Scrapnook we are utilizing the technology’s ability to store these images pristinely, for generations, and its ability to track ownership of each NFT to its origins.   • Some technical info on the process.

    We will be minting the NFTs onto the Solana blockchain. This network is popular related of its affordability, speed, and scalability. Many talented coders are working on the Solana blockchain and positioning our NFTs in this ecosystem will give our clients the best chance to get the most from their NFTs. We use well-supported libraries to mint onto the blockchain and custom-made software to write all the data needed to interact with these libraries. We use Arweave storage solutions along with our smart contract to complete the NFT package. We plan to get our process audited by third-party specialists to ensure our workflow is flawless and remains up to date. Until then we are minting the NFTs as mutable. This means that if there are any issues with the process, we can fix the NFT quickly and easily. Once our process has been audited, we will lock the existing NFTs and mint future NFTs and non-mutable.
