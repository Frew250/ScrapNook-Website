Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the Blockchain?

    First of all there is a blockhain for each crypto currency. Each blockchain is a list of transactions grouped in blocks, and linked together by hashes. Each transactions is a simple send and recieve transaction like account A sent account B $5. All the data on this blockchain is duplicated and distributed accross multiple systems making it impossible to change, hack or cheat the system since there is more than one source of truth. Most blockchains are public and every transactions can be traced back to its origin wallet.

  • What is an NFT?

    NFT stands for Non-Fungible-Token. Some very smart people in 2014, figured out that they could write some code and insert some data into the blockchain. They linked that piece of data to one of their homemade videos (photo and video files can be used). This action is called minting. By minting this video onto the blockchain it meant that anyone who had their address could look it up and play the video. They in essence published this video online with a signature of ownership very much like an artist displays work in a gallery with their signature on the bottom.
